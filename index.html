PPC

A production possibilities curve is a graphical representation of the alternative combinations of goods and services an economy can produce. It illustrates the production possibilities model. In drawing the production possibilities curve, we shall assume that the economy can produce only two goods and that the quantities of factors of production and the technology available to the economy are fixed.The PPC is graphed for two goods. One cannot Graph ‘Television’ against ’Wheat’ production cause both goods are made from different resources. Although one can graph ’Wheat’ production against ‘Tomato’ production since both require land to get produced. Thus it’s imperative to compare goods that utilize the same resources because the PPC illustrates that as “production of one good increases, production of the other good must decrease due to scarce resources”. What that means is a company or a country is able to produce more of one good only if it produces less of another. Countries and companies can use it to decide how to effectively and efficiently allocate resources to achieve maximum resource efficiency and to generate the most profitable product mix.
